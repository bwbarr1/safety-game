Direct Controls

Immediate actions to manage risk (seatbelt, spotter, wearing PPE correctly). Pros: Instant. Cons: Relies on behavior.

Engineering Controls

Design features that remove or reduce the hazard (guards, interlocks, lane‑keep assist). Pros: Reliable. Cons: May require investment.

Administrative Controls

Procedures/training that influence how work is done (signage, SOPs, checklists). Pros: Scalable. Cons: Requires adherence.

Better Than Nothing

Not ideal, but improves awareness when higher‑order controls aren’t available. Aim for the strongest control available. Better Than Nothing is a last resort.
